Zone 2 Heart Rate Calculator

This calculator uses the Karvonen Method to determine heart rate zones.1

The Karvonen Method, or Heart Rate Reserve method, was developed by Dr. Martti Karvonen from Finland. It calculates training intensities by considering both your maximum heart rate (MHR) and your resting heart rate (RHR). By taking into account your RHR, this method provides a more individualized measure of exercise intensity.1

Zone 2 training, often referred to as aerobic or endurance training, is instrumental in building an athlete's aerobic capacity. It helps in improving the efficiency of the cardiovascular system, allowing the body to transport and use oxygen more effectively during exercise. This training zone also promotes fat metabolism, increases capillary density in muscles, and aids in the development and maintenance of a strong aerobic base, which is crucial for supporting higher-intensity efforts. Such adaptations can lead to enhanced endurance, better exercise recovery, and a lower risk of overtraining. Over time, consistent Zone 2 training can contribute to improvements in both short-duration, high-intensity events and longer endurance races34.
